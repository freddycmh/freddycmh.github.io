Brooklyn Bridge

Huge crowds gathered on May 24, 1883, to watch the bridge’s opening ceremony, which The New York Times described, in reference to Brooklyn, as “the greatest gala day in the history of that moral suburb.”

Activities

This treasure of park spans over 1.3 miles—and offers enchanting views of the New York Harbor and Lower Manhattan. New Yorkers love to go jogging here or take a walk at sunset, while tourists like to get their fill of photos and excitement.

Explore Dumbo Ladies and gentlemen welcome to one of the most beautiful neighborhoods in the city: DUMBO or Dumbo. Maybe you are wondering what Dumbo stands for, well, it’s an acronym for Down Under the Manhattan Bridge Overpass.

Visit the Brooklyn Historical Society DUMBO If you want to get into a really interesting museum, we highly recommend you go to the Brooklyn Historical Society. Here you’ll experience a multimedia journey that brings to life the history of the Brooklyn coast through the voices of people who have lived in this area of New York over time.

One World Trade Center In Lower Manhattan, not far from the Brooklyn Bridge, you’ll find the One World Trade Center (about 0.5 miles away).

Explore the original commercial hub of New York The Seaport District is the most vibrant neighborhoods in New York and is located along the East River with front-row views of the city skyline and the wonderful Brooklyn Bridge.
